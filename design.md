# Kai Design Direction

## Product Feel

Kai should feel like a teen utility, not an AI landing page. The default impression is: calm, private, slightly cool, and useful after school when someone has five minutes and does not want a lecture.

The app should avoid generic AI tropes: glowing bots, gradient blobs, “journey” language, oversized marketing cards, fake productivity dashboards, and corporate wellness copy.

## Visual System

- Shape: tight 8px radius, compact panels, no nested cards.
- Color: warm off-white base, dark ink surfaces, citrus/lime highlights, muted blue and green action states, coral for attention. Avoid one-note purple/blue gradients.
- Type: bold app headings, short labels, no hero-scale type inside panels.
- Texture: subtle grid/noise only; no decorative orbs.
- Layout: mobile app first. Bottom nav on phones, compact top nav on desktop.
- Motion: small hover/tap shifts only. No bouncy mascot behavior.

## Voice

Kai should sound like a grounded older sibling. Short, specific, and never therapeutic unless a safety route takes over.

Good:
- “Pick one small rep.”
- “What’s actually loud right now?”
- “Body, goals, or reset?”

Bad:
- “Transform your wellness journey.”
- “Unlock your potential.”
- “I’m your AI companion.”
- “Let’s level up your mental health.”

## Screen Priorities

The first screen should show the app, not explain the app:

1. Kai check-in surface.
2. Three clear modes: Body, Goals, Reset.
3. Today’s plan with believable micro-actions.
4. Progress shown as low-pressure evidence, not gamified pressure.
5. Safety available, but not presented like a hospital banner unless needed.

## Demo Standard

Every visible panel needs to look populated with real state: times, streaks, suggested next action, recent check-ins, and concrete labels. Avoid empty placeholder cards.

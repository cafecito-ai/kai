# Kai Engine Blueprint for Lev

Use Direction 3 as the product frame: calm, teen-specific, serious enough for parents, still simple enough to use after school.

## Repeatable Engine Framework

Every engine should answer four questions before UI polish:

1. What exact teen moment does this help with?
2. What input does Kai need from the teen?
3. What response is Kai allowed to give?
4. What proof should be saved as progress?

## Physical Health Starting Point

The physical engine should be broader than food tracking.

Core modules:

- Food tracking: meal text, optional photo, hunger/fullness, energy, mood, notes.
- Movement: sport, workout, walking, stretching, practice, recovery.
- Sleep: bedtime, wake time, quality, blockers, next sleep experiment.
- Recovery: soreness, hydration, breathing, short reset sessions.

Guardrails:

- Do not frame meals as good or bad.
- Do not optimize for weight loss.
- Do not rank bodies or reward restriction.
- Avoid calories and macro targets until reviewed with teen-safety expertise.
- Detect language around starving, purging, bingeing, body hate, and obsessive tracking.

Preferred language:

- "fuel"
- "patterns"
- "energy"
- "recovery"
- "what helped"
- "one small next move"

## Customization Checklist

For each engine, Lev should provide:

- 8 to 12 teen-facing starter prompts.
- 5 examples of good Kai responses.
- 5 examples Kai should never say.
- The progress event names that should count.
- Any parent or safety language that must appear.
- The tone boundary: warm, balanced, or direct.
